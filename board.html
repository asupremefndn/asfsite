About Keisha
Keisha is serving a second term as president of the A-Supreme Foundation. An advocate for honouring the dignity of human life and empowering others to thrive, Keisha has championed the mandate of the A-supreme Foundation to improve the quality of life for seniors. In 2017 she coined the Buy Some Time campaign through which donors finance in-home care for seniors unable to care for themselves. A communications strategist with 15+ years’ experience in media and corporate communications, Keisha is a director at Global Media & Communications Corporation and is a magazine publisher, book editor, columnist and digital media producer and host.
About Henderson
Henderson Ifill is serving the A-Supreme Board for the second year and chairs the Fundraising committee. He brings an impressive track record of community engagement and leadership to the foundation. He has served as president of the Malton Neighbourhood Services, a multi-purpose community service offering transition services to seniors and adult newcomers; president of the Malton Cricket Camp & Sports Club; vice president of the Toronto District Cricket Association and has also managed the Canada National Senior Cricket Team. In his professional capacity, Henderson serves as the general manager of an engineering company in Toronto.
About Mackeda
Mackeda has served on the A-Supreme Foundation board since 2014. She is a legal consultant and aspiring entrepreneur in Toronto with over ten years’ experience in the legal and employment industry in Canada and Australia. Whilst in law school, Mackeda founded the wellness for lawyer’s association; she has presented on and participated in research dealing with mental wellness for lawyers; is a facilitator of start-up workshops for young entrepreneurs and has volunteered with the City of Toronto Youth Employment Services. Mackeda holds a degree in criminal justice, Juris Doctor degree in law and a Graduate Diploma in Legal Practice. An advocate for the empowerment and upliftment of people, Mackeda chairs the membership committee and also serves on the outreach committee and coordinates the Foundation’s legal workshops for seniors.
About Clive
Clive has served on the board of A-Supreme Foundation since 2014. An entrepreneur with over 30 years’ experience working in the business and the financial sector, he has extensive knowledge in the accounting and financial sector, handling both business and personal matters. A strong advocate for the under-served and under-represented communities locally and in the Caribbean diaspora, Clive has served on several boards across the GTA, most notably, the A-Supreme Foundation, Community Justice Alternatives of Durham Region, and The Afri-Can FoodBasket.
About Desiree
Desiree King is the Founder & CEO of Senior Living 411 Inc., in Ontario Canada. Her service began at age 14, when under the guidance of her mother, volunteered as a Candy Striper at a local hospital delivering books in the Geriatric ward. Over the past 30 years, she has operated several businesses serving seniors, including a Prescription Delivery company, Senior Real Estate Specialist (SRES), and provided business development for a leading Home Health Care company. She is a Certified Senior Housing Professional, certified Senior Home Safety SpecialistTM, Certified Aging-In- Place Specialist, Gentle Persuasion Approach in Dementia Care Certified (GPA), Distinguished Toastmaster (DTM), and an Advisor Member with Age Safe® Canada.
About Tiney
Desiree has over 20 years’ experience in IT specializing in executive support for members of the accounting, government, legal & pharmaceutical sectors. In the last five years she transitioned to real estate and focuses on serving seniors. Her passion for working with the elderly emerged from her early years working as a Candy Striper and Dietary Aide in several Toronto area hospitals, to owning a company that delivered prescription medication primarily to seniors. She holds several designations including Senior Real Estate Specialist (SRES), Certified Aging-In-Place Specialist (CAPS), Certified Senior Housing Specialist (CSHP) & Accredited Senior Specialist (ASA). Desiree is also a Distinguished Toastmaster and has served as Division leader and Area leader with Toastmasters International. Prior to this she was instrumental is forming a new Toastmaster Club where she served as VP of Education.
About Keith

He is a pastor a Revival Time Tabernacle Worldwide Ministries in Toronto and is an accountant with over 35 years of public and private sector experience. Mr. McCormack has extensive board experience having severed on numerous boards throughout his career.

About Jerrold
